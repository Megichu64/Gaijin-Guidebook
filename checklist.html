Phase 1: The Essentials (Pre-Flight)

Tip: Ensure it is valid for your entire length of stay. Japan does not strictly require "6 months validity," but airlines might.

Action: Complete Immigration & Customs forms before you fly. Save the QR code to "Offline Mode" or screenshot it.

Tip: Keep a digital copy accessible offline.

Requirement: Immigration often requires the specific address of your first night's stay. Wifi might not work immediately, so print this page!

Warning: Stimulants (Adderall, Vyvanse) are strictly illegal. Bring a prescription copy for permitted meds. If bringing >1 month supply, apply for a Yunyu Kakunin-sho 3 weeks early.

Tip: Medical care in Japan is affordable but requires upfront payment without insurance.

Rec: Ubigi or Airalo for eSIMs. NinjaWifi for physical devices.

Phase 2: The Carry-On (On the Plane)

Tip: Keep these in an easily accessible front pocket, not buried in your bag.

Install: Google Maps, Google Translate, and Suica (iPhone only). Android users: get a physical card in Japan.

Critical: Google Maps drains battery fast. You will need this daily.

Tip: Noise-canceling is a lifesaver on the 10+ hour flight.

Comfort: Airline pillows are thin. Bring your own for better sleep.

Tip: Fill it up after security. Staying hydrated helps fight jet lag.

Health: Wear these on the plane to prevent swelling and DVT during the long flight.

Pack: Toothbrush, deodorant, lip balm. Freshening up before landing makes a huge difference.

Important: Japan has almost NO public trash cans. This bag will double as your personal trash bag during the day.

Phase 3: The Checked Bag (Main Luggage)

Reality Check: You will walk 15,000+ steps a day. Do not bring new shoes you haven't broken in!

Tip: For easy removal at temples, ryokans, and fitting rooms. Laces are a pain.

Tip: Japan has distinct seasons. Summer is humid; Winter is cold. Layers are your friend.

Crucial: Most Japanese outlets do not have the 3rd grounding hole. Your laptop charger won't fit without this adapter.

Tip: For carrying souvenirs (snacks, anime goods) home. Check a "Don Quijote" store if you forget to bring one.

Phase 4: Japan Pick-Ups

Where: Get these at the airport ticket machines. Essential for trains, convenience stores, and vending machines.

Warning: Prices hiked in late 2023. Use a "JR Pass Calculator" online. Buying individual tickets is often 40% cheaper now.

Advice: Bring ~$65 USD worth. You need this for the initial train ticket and loading your IC card if your credit card fails at the kiosk.

Tip: Japan is still cash-heavy. You will accumulate heavy coins quickly.

Surprise: Public restrooms rarely have paper towels, and park restrooms often lack soap.
